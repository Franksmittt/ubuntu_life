WHAT UBUNTU LIFE RESOURCES ACTUALLY IS (plain English)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Ubuntu Life Resources is NOT a factory and NOT “random consulting.”

It is a commercial go-to-market and execution partner for Africa:

• It represents trusted international suppliers and helps them SELL, DELIVER,
  and SCALE in Southern Africa and selected Sub-Saharan markets.
• It works with governments, large institutions, and private companies that
  need compliant supply, technical credibility, and local access.
• It combines: market access + stakeholder engagement + sales pipeline work +
  deal structuring + supplier coordination + cross-border trade facilitation.

Think: “distribution + representation + structured supply” for products that
fit the company’s three pillars: agricultural biosecurity, safe water, and
food security (high-protein shelf-stable foods).


WHY THIS CAN FEEL CONFUSING
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The role sits BETWEEN brands:

• Ubuntu Life Resources presents and sells solutions in its territories.
• Scientific Sanitation Solutions (SSS) is the technology / sanitation supplier
  behind SANI-99™ for AGRI and SANI-AMANZI water treatment (see sales pitch:
  “Strategic Agents for SSS (Scientific Sanitation Solutions)”).
• Tonno Bonno International is a separate food brand (canned seafood). The
  Tonno Bonno company profile lists Ubuntu Life Resources as the “Agent” for
  South Africa, Botswana, Nigeria, Mozambique, and Middle East operations.

So Sanchia’s “job” is: open doors, qualify buyers, run the commercial narrative,
align with regulations and procurement, and make sure product stories are
backed by evidence (lab reports, field tests, case links).


THREE SOLUTION PILLARS (what you sell / represent)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

1) AGRICULTURAL BIOSECURITY / SANI-99™ FOR AGRI (via SSS)
   Positioning: veterinary-grade / farm-grade disinfection for pathogen control
   on farms, in transport, and in food production environments.

   Storyline from sales pitch PDF:
   • Targets risks such as avian influenza, Newcastle disease, Salmonella,
     E. coli, foot-and-mouth context (see FOOT AND MOUTH DISEASE BROCHURE.pdf
     and 52871gon6319.pdf for regulatory / biosecurity framing).
   • Claims to support log-reduction performance, residual protection claims,
     concentrated powder and sachet formats, DEFRA / BEIC / PCS and BS EN /
     SANS references as cited in the pitch. Always cite the official supplier
     sheet when making compliance statements on the website.

   Evidence in knowledge/ (use on a “Proof & downloads” or portfolio page):
   • Sani-99_Agri.pdf, product / agri positioning
   • CPT SANI-AMANZI 2022.pdf, KZN SANI-AMANZI test 2022.pdf, regional water /
     sanitation testing context (customer named as Scientific Sanitation
     Solutions on certificates)
   • Field / before-after: Mooikloof Borehole Before/After, Mooikloof stream
     before/after, Riverton Before and After, SANI-AMANZI SMT Before and After
     Hennops River.pdf, SANI-AMANZI before and after test 2020.pdf,
     SANI-AMANZI field and lab test 15-Jan-2021.pdf,
     SANI-AMANZI Certified Class 1 Good quality water.pdf

2) WATER PURIFICATION / SANI-AMANZI (via SSS)
   Positioning: powder-based treatment for contaminated water in rural, bulk,
   and off-grid settings. Coagulation, flocculation, sedimentation, then
   filtration of the top layer (see SANI_AMANZI_Technical_Guidelines.pdf).

   Practical protocol (from technical guidelines extract):
   • Dosage example: 6 g per 20 L; scales linearly (e.g. 300 g / 1000 L).
   • Steps: collect water → dose → stir → stand ≥30 min → draw from top layer
     → filter through dense cloth.
   • Limitations called out: boreholes (do not dose in situ), JoJo tanks
     (mixing / sludge issues, treat in containers first).

   Strong lab narrative:
   • 170022-Water Purification-16-Nov-2023.pdf, Aquatico report for Scientific
     Sanitation Solutions; raw vs treated time series (supports “before /
     after” storytelling on a portfolio or case-study page).

3) FOOD SECURITY / TONNO BONNO INTERNATIONAL (high-protein canned seafood)
   Positioning: FMCG seafood (pilchards, sardines, tuna) for African and export
   markets; Ubuntu Life Resources listed as distribution / agent partner in the
   TonnoBonno Company Profile Updated.pdf.

   Website angle: institutional buyers, wholesalers, government feeding
   programmes, and retail distributors. Not “Ubuntu manufactures tuna,” but
   “Ubuntu represents Tonno Bonno for market growth and supply in its
   territories.”
