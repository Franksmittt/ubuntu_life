Agri biosecurity

Sanitation, contamination control, and disease-risk reduction for farms and agri value chains, always aligned to registered uses and local rules.

This pillar

Where farm programmes meet compliant supply

Ubuntu Life Resources represents approved international suppliers in Southern Africa. We qualify buyers, map channels, bring technical stakeholders into the conversation, and coordinate compliant supply. We are not the factory; we are your Africa-facing sales and execution interface.

Farm hygiene, animal health positioning, and crop-care pathways belong here when the underlying chemistry, labels, and import rules match the programme you are briefing.

How we work with you

Structured support, not a product dump

Map channels and SOPs

We work with your biosecurity reality: where animals move, how trucks and crates are washed, and what documentation a buyer or integrator will actually audit.

Regulatory and label fit

Conversations are grounded in registered use and claim discipline, so programmes do not get stranded between sales promises and on-farm law.

Traceable replenishment

We brief supply for programmes that need named brands and repeatable replenishment, not one-off opportunistic loads.

Representative line

SANI-99™ for AGRI

We support structured enquiry and supply for SANI-99™ for AGRI (organic iodine complex) where registration and label claims match your channel or programme. See the SANI-99™ for AGRI product page for formats and pathogen scope, then reach us through the contact form for technical sheets and dosing guidance.

Technical fit

Route questions on chemistry, application context, and documentation so decisions are not made on a brochure alone.

Dosing and SOP support

We connect you to the right technical pack and language for implementers, procurement, and the farm gate.

Channel introductions

When it helps, we open conversations with the right commercial and programme contacts, without anonymous marketplace noise.

Who this is for

Farms and integrators

Commercial farms, integrators, and cooperatives that are serious about strengthening biosecurity SOPs and audit trails.

Agri processing

Processors and exporters tightening sanitation controls in line with customer and regulator expectations.

Documented supply

Implementers that need traceable, named product supply rather than informal or anonymous sourcing.
